Overview

        Agumbe is a high-altitude village located in Shimoga district in the malnad region of Karnataka. Agumbe is surrounded by rich biodiversity and the hill station borders one of the last surviving lowland rain forests. It is called "The Cherrapunji of the South" due to extreme rainfall here. During monsoon, this place gets transformed into a breathtaking spectacle as it gets covered by lush green curtain etched with vigorous rivers. Amidst the picturesque beauty of Western ghats,  there lies a plethora of tourist places that attracts hundreds of people from all over the country.

For all the trekking enthusiasts out there, Agumbe presents to you some excellent peaks. Narasimha parvatha (1150 m above sea level) is one of the highest peaks around here, which shall not fail to surprise you with its hidden treasures of natural beauty. A trek to kudlu-teertha falls and Onake abbi falls pays you back with the cold dip in fascinating waterfalls. Similarly, Kundadri hills, Barkana falls and Jogi gundi falls are all must visit places with their spectacular scenery to treat your eyes with!
