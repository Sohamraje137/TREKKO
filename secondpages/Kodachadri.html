Overview

        The outskirts of Karnataka are gifted with numerous peaks and waterfalls.Shimoga is one such place,located close to Bangalore. The destination rests peacefully on the banks of the Tunga River, making up for its soothing and salubrious climate. In the varied beauties of this awesome destination lies the Kodachadri Peak.Kodachadri lies 115 km from Shimoga, 20 km from Kollur and 500 km away from Bengalaru. It which boasts of lush green forests on its summit.The peak also holds the distinction of being a Natural Heritage sight as declared by the government of Karnataka. Nestled amidst the Western Ghats of Karnataka,Kodachadri is located at 1,343 meters above the sea level.
Designated as the 10th tallest peak in the state and a precious natural heritage location, Kodachadri trek boasts of compact forests, beautiful waterfalls en route gorgeous jungle trails and picturesque landscapes.

The name Kodachadri comes from the Kannada words 'Kodacha' or 'Kodashi', which means hill of Kutaja flowers and 'Adri' a Sanskrit word which means hill,both these combined together derived the word Kodachadri.Kutaja in Sanskrit means Girimallika or Jasmine of the hill. It is also called 'Kodashi Parvatha'.The peak of Kodachadri is perfect canvas like backdrop to the famous Mookambika Temple and also the Mookambika National Park.
Being in the heart of a National Park there are various opportunities for trekking and wildlife enthusiasts.
Kodachadri is the home for a stunning range of Biodiversity such as Malabar Langoor, Malabar Pied Hornbill and Indian Rock Python among many others.Some of the interesting places to visit in Kodachadri are Sarvajina Peetam, Sri Mookambika Devi Temple, Hidlumane Waterfalls, Sauparnika River, Arasinagundi Wateralls, Belakallu theertha Waterfalls, Nagara Fort, Kodachadri Trekking Route and Mookambika Wildlife Sanctuary.September to February is ideal season for trekking.
There are different trekking routes,one is from Nittur and on the way one can find Hidulmane falls.The another one is from Karaghatta which is through the rugged wild terrain. On this route one can find two small shrines where vedic rituals are performed. The presiding deity in the fist shrine is worshipped in her terrific form, one can see a Trishul with which Devi killed Mookasura and in the second, the diety is worshipped in her beautiful and merciful form - Goddess Uma. There is a Ganapati Cave with an ancient idol of Lord Ganesha and behind the idol, there is a tunnel, which leads to Kollur.
Further up, on the hilltop is the Sarvajnapeetam is situated. Sarvajnapeetam is a rock structure where Idol of Sri Sankaracharya worshiped.This is the place where he is said to have immersed himself into deep meditation and received the ultimate knowledge and dissolved into the universe.The entire structure is made of rock and its roof has various yantras and chakras carved and resembles a 'Meru' - a three dimensional form of the mystic design called the 'Srichakram'. One has to descent steeply on the western side of the hill to reach a holy cave called Chitramoola were Sri Sankaracharya did meditation to please goddess Mookambika.From karikatte gate is another treking route which one can start trek without a guide as path is well defined inside the forest. The distance of trek from Karikatte gate to the peak is around 12-14kms. Kodachadri is one of the best places to de-stress yourself completely. Also don't forget to witness the spectacular sight of sunrise and sunset. Kodachadri is truely a heaven on Earth!
